# Stabilization Protocols

Based on: "Basic triage in dogs and cats: Part I-III" (Thomovsky E, Ilie L; Can Vet J 2024)

## 1. Acute Respiratory Distress (Part I)

### Step 1: Oxygen
- Provide oxygen with minimal stress.
- Methods: Flow-by, oxygen cage/chamber, oxygen tent (plastic wrap), intranasal cannula (large dogs).
- Flow-by: 100% O2 from anesthesia circuit or oxygen tank.

### Step 2: Categorize Type
- **Upper Airway (Stridor/Stertor):**
  - Causes: Brachycephalic airway, laryngeal paralysis, collapsing trachea, oropharyngeal mass.
  - Stabilization: Sedation (Butorphanol 0.2-0.5 mg/kg IV/IM +/- Acepromazine 0.01-0.2 mg/kg IV/IM), Endotracheal intubation if needed.
- **Lower Airway (Dyspnea, Cyanosis, "Loud" or "Quiet" auscultation):**
  - "Loud" (Crackles/Wheezes): Pulmonary parenchyma involvement (Edema, Pneumonia, Feline Asthma).
    - Edema: Furosemide (2-10 mg/kg IV/IM in dogs/cats).
    - Asthma: Oxygen + Bronchodilators (Terbutaline 0.01 mg/kg SC or IM, or Albuterol inhaler).
  - "Quiet": Pneumothorax or Pleural Effusion.
    - Action: Thoracocentesis (refer to Box 1 in Part I).

---

## 2. Seizures and Cluster Seizures (Part I)

### Step 1: Is the animal having a seizure NOW?
- YES: Administer Benzodiazepine.
  - **IV catheter present:** Diazepam or Midazolam 0.5 mg/kg IV.
  - **No IV catheter:** Midazolam 1 mg/kg IN (intranasal) or 2 mg/kg rectal/IM.
  - Repeat doses as needed every 2-3 min (max 3 times).

### Step 2: Second-line Anti-epileptics (if refractory)
- Levetiracetam: 60 mg/kg IV loading dose.
- Phenobarbital: 16 mg/kg loading dose (4 doses of 4 mg/kg every 0.5-4 h).
- Propofol: 1-3.5 mg/kg up to 6 mg/kg slow IV, followed by CRI (0.1 to 0.25 mg/kg/min).

---

## 3. Acute Bleeding (Part II)

### Step 1: Stop the bleeding
- **Option 1: Pressure wrap / Tourniquet.**
  - Apply pressure wrap directly to the site.
  - Tourniquet for limb/tail if life-threatening.
- **Option 2: Hemostasis.**
  - Sedation: Acepromazine or Dexmedetomidine to lower peripheral blood pressure.
  - Hemostatic agents: Chitin/Chitosan, Zeolite, QuikClot.

---

## 4. Cardiovascular Stabilization (Part II & III)

### Shock and Resuscitation
- **Goal:** Restore vital signs (respiration, color, heart rate, pulses).
- Fluids: Isotonic crystalloids (1/4 to 1/3 of shock dose: 90 mL/kg for dogs, 45-60 mL/kg for cats).
- End points: Normal HR, strong pulses, lactate < 2.0 mmol/L.

### Arrhythmias
- **Tachyarrhythmias:**
  - Dogs: Lidocaine 2 mg/kg IV (slow).
  - Cats: Diltiazem 0.1 mg/kg IV or Sotalol (careful).
- **Bradyarrhythmias:**
  - Atropine 0.04 mg/kg IV/IM if hemodynamically unstable.
