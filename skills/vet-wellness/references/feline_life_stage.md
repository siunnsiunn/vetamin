# Feline Life Stage Guidelines (AAHA/AAFP 2021)

## Life Stage Definitions
- **Kitten**: Birth up to 1 year.
- **Young Adult**: 1 through 6 years.
- **Mature Adult**: 7 to 10 years.
- **Senior**: Aged over 10 years.
- **End-of-life**: Any age.

## Key Focus per Life Stage
- **Kitten**: Congenital defects, nutrition/weaning, socialization (period closes 9-10 weeks), behavior modification (positive reinforcement).
- **Young Adult**: Obesity prevention, dental health (70% have disease by age 2), indoor/outdoor risk assessment.
- **Mature Adult**: Early detection of CKD, hyperthyroidism, dental disease, OA screening.
- **Senior**: Vigilance for comorbidities, mobility changes, sensory decline (vision/hearing), cognitive dysfunction.

## History Taking: "Provocative Questions"
- "Is your cat happy?"
- "Has there been any urination or defecation outside the litter box?"
- "Describe your cat's diet and daily eating habits?"
- "What's the highest surface your cat will jump to?"

## Environmental Needs (5 Pillars)
1. Safe place to hide.
2. Multiple, separated key resources (food, water, litter, scratching, sleeping).
3. Opportunity for play/predatory behavior.
4. Positive, consistent, predictable human-cat social interaction.
5. Environment that respects the cat's sense of smell.
