# Canine Life Stage Guidelines (AAHA 2019)

## Life Stage Definitions
- **Puppy**: Birth to cessation of rapid growth (~6-9 months, varying with breed).
- **Young Adult**: Cessation of rapid growth to completion of physical and social maturation (usually 3-4 years).
- **Mature Adult**: Completion of physical and social maturation until the last 25% of estimated lifespan.
- **Senior**: The last 25% of estimated lifespan through end of life.

## Physical Exam (PE) Focus
- **Puppy**: Congenital disorders, deciduous dentition, behavior/socialization.
- **Young Adult**: Congenital disorders, weight baseline, musculoskeletal/mobility.
- **Mature Adult**: Early disease detection, trends in vitals, OA screening (collect owner observations).
- **Senior**: Body mapping, comorbidities, mobility/OA, vision/hearing adaptations, cognitive changes.

## 5 Vital Assessments (Required at every visit)
1. Temperature
2. Pulse
3. Respiration
4. Pain
5. Nutrition (Body Weight, BCS, MCS)

## History Taking Items (Key Discussion Points)
- **Lifestyle**: Home environment, interactions, wildlife exposure, housing/confinement.
- **Zoonoses**: Parasite control (hookworms, roundworms), rabies, Leptospira.
- **Behavior**: Training classes, cognitive dysfunction (seniors), anxiety/phobias.
- **Nutrition**: Feeding schedule, BCS/MCS trends, supplements.
- **Reproduction**: Sterilization timing (Small breeds: ~6mo; Large breeds: Wait until growth stops 9-15mo for males).
