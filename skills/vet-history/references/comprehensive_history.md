# Comprehensive History Taking (Calgary-Cambridge Framework)

## 1. Initiation
- **Greeting**: Identify yourself and the client/patient.
- **Set Agenda**: "What would you like to discuss today?"
- **Summarize Early**: Repeat back the main concerns to ensure alignment.

## 2. Information Gathering
- **The "Funnel" Sequence**:
  - Start with **Open-ended questions**: "Tell me more about [symptom]..." or "Can you describe his daily routine?"
  - Follow with **Closed questions** for specifics: "Did he vomit today?" or "Is he drinking more than usual?"
- **Chunking and Checking**: Give small amounts of information and check for understanding. "Does that make sense to you?"

## 3. The "Provocative" Questions (Deep Dives)
- **General Wellbeing**: "Is your pet happy?"
- **Hydration**: "How often does your pet drink water?" (Better than "Is he drinking normally?")
- **Diet**: "Can you describe his daily eating habits and the specific food brand?"
- **Elimination**: "Has there been any urination or defecation outside the litter box/usual spot?"
- **Pain/Mobility**: "When was the last time you saw him stretch and yawn luxuriously?" or "What is the highest surface he will jump to?"

## 4. Client Perspective
- **Patient's Role**: Is this a companion, working dog, or service animal?
- **Goals of Care**: "What are your hopes for today's visit?"
- **Financial/Practical Budgets**: Financial, Time, Emotional, and Physical.
