# Emergency Triage History Taking

## Immediate Assessment (Rapid History)
When a patient arrives in potential crisis, ask these critical questions immediately:

1.  **Reason for Visit**: "What is the primary concern today?"
2.  **Onset**: "When did this start? Was it sudden or gradual?"
3.  **Breathing**: "Have you noticed any difficulty breathing or unusual noises while breathing?"
4.  **Circulation/Bleeding**: "Is there any active bleeding or has the animal collapsed?"
5.  **Seizures**: "Is the animal having a seizure now or has it had one recently? How long did it last?"
6.  **Toxin/Trauma**: "Is there a possibility of toxin ingestion (plants, meds, foods) or recent trauma?"

## The "Mnemonic" Approach
- **A (Airway)**: Noise? Stridor?
- **B (Breathing)**: Effort? Rate?
- **C (Circulation)**: Pulse quality? Color? (Ask owner about gums if remote).
- **D (Disability/CNS)**: Level of consciousness? Seizures?
- **E (Exposure)**: Toxins? Temperature extremes?

## Triage-Specific Questions by System
- **Respiratory**: Coughing vs. Dyspnea? Productive? Orthopnea (neck extended)?
- **Gastrointestinal**: Retching without vomiting (GDV concern)? Frequency of vomiting/diarrhea?
- **Neurological**: Ability to walk? Mentation changes? Cluster seizures?
- **Obstetrical**: Active labor duration? Visible fetal parts?
