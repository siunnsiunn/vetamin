# Colorado State University (CSU) Acute Pain Scale

## Canine Acute (Score 0-4)
- **0 (Minimal)**: Happy, content, nontender to palpation.
- **1 (Mild)**: Content to slightly unsettled, reacts to palpation by looking around/flinching.
- **2 (Mild to Moderate)**: Looks uncomfortable, may whimper/lick wound, flinches/guards when touched.
- **3 (Moderate)**: Unsettled, crying, unwilling to move, dramatic response to palpation (bite threat).
- **4 (Moderate to Severe)**: Unresponsive or screaming, aggressive to palpation, rigid body tension.

## Feline Acute (Score 0-4)
- **0 (Minimal)**: Content and quiet, interested in surroundings, nontender.
- **1 (Mild)**: Signs subtle (withdrawal), less interested in surroundings, may/may not react to palpation.
- **2 (Mild to Moderate)**: Lays curled up/tucked, hair coat appears rough, responds aggressively or tries to escape palpation.
- **3 (Moderate)**: Constantly yowling/growling, unlikely to move, growls/hisses at non-painful palpation.
- **4 (Moderate to Severe)**: Prostrate, potentially unresponsive, rigid to avoid painful movement.
